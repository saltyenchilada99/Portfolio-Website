John F Kennedy

(1917-1963)

Childhood

John Fitzgerald Kennedy was born in Brookline Massachusetts on May 29 1917. He had eight siblings and went to Harvard University to study Government, earning his bachelor's of arts degree in 1940

Military

On September 24th 1941, two months before pearl harbor, John joined the United States Naval Reserve. In 1942, he completed training at the Naval Reserve Officer Training School where he would eventually be assigned to active duty in the South Pacific. He was awarded the Navy and Marines Corps Medal for his service and heroism

Government

After the war, he decided to go into politics, winning his running district in the 1946 House of Representatives election. Afterwards, he ran for senator in the 1952 election, beating senator incumbant Henry Cabot Lodge Jr. He eventually won the 1960 presidential election against Richard Nixon, becoming the 35th president of the United States

Cuba

The president's first attempt to stop nuclear tensions in Cuba was an aggressive tactic that would go on to be known as the Bay of Pigs invasion. The invasion attempt was unsuccessful leaving 114 soldiers dead and the rest imprisoned. Later in 1962 the Kennedy administration found out that nuclear missile sites were being built in Cuba, marking a high point in the cold war. This would go on to be known as the Cuban Missile Crisis, consisting of naval tensions between Cuba and the United States. After two weeks stalling, the U.S government made a deal with Cuba to dismantle the missile sites with the promise of the Cuba not being attacked by its neighbor from the north

Assassination

On November 22 1963, John visited Texas to settle political affairs. He was driving through Dallas on a limousine convertabile when he was shot once in the back and once in the head by Lee Harvey Oswald. Oswald was arrested, and killed several days later
